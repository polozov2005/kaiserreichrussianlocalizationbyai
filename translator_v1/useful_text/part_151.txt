A Day of Freedom, A Day of Reunion
Shan State has finally gotten enough of the Chinese dogs. The saophas are angered, and so are the people. We are called to arms. Today is a day of independence and of reunion with our Tai brothers. Men, women and children of Shan, rise up!
Centralise the States
The system of federalism in Shan is anachronistic and requires change. The saophas lay at the command of Sao Shwe Thaik, and the Confederation must become centralised if we are to advance in the world.
The Federalist Dream
The Status Quo for Shan has worked quite well over the past centuries. United we are in a decentralised federation, each state ruled by its own Saopha, and it shall be so forevermore.
Reduce the Saophas' Authority
It is time to take the question up to the saophas. They must bend the knee and capitulate for the greater good. We must start by reducing their authority over their states, and allowing Taunggyi more control over the various regions of Shan.
Proclaim the New Government
At Taunggyi we will proclaim a new government. We are one country, with one capital and one government, the saophas retaining some of their power in the new reformed political system.
The Guiding Hand of Sao Shwe Thaik
The Tai people are loyal subjects of the government in Taunggyi, of their regions, and of Sao Shwe Thaik. Sao Shwe Thaik will guide Shan to centralisation and democracy, and the people will follow.
Putting the Nation First
Too many put their clan above all else. Such loyalties are poisonous to national unity and allow foreign elements to play our people against each other. A broad propaganda campaign should be initiated to discourage such ties.
Fight for Freedom
The struggle for independence was long and bloody. But we have overcome it, and now we can apply its lesson formally to the development of the armed forces.
A Professional National Army
The decentralised structure of our forces up to this point cannot be maintained. Let us develop not only a western style professional force where every man is drilled extensively and atuned to a rigid system of command.
Superior Weaponry
What we lack in modern machines of war, we make up for with fighting spirit. Therefore, our men must be able to fight to the best of there ability. To accomplish that, we should study foreign arms development and copy effective advanced foreign designs.
The Special Forces Project
We will never have the numbers to stand toe to toe with the greatest powers of the world. However, highly skilled specialised units can be our great equaliser.
To Preserve Peace
To preserve peace, we must be ready to make war. The entire citizenry must should be required to provide a small percentage of its income or harvest to the armed forces so that they may never falter.
The National Arsenal
The nations of the west have central military arsenals which engage in both production and research. We must have one of our own.
To the Factories
We should expand the military provisions programme to also extend to enterprises.
The Iron Railway
The nation's iron mines are connected only by dirt roads trafficked by mules. Though the terrain is extremely harsh, using modern methods, we can at last fully connect them with the cities.
Local Defence
The armed forces should recognise the unique structure of our nation. Individual states should have authority over their own sections of the army and held responsible for recruitment.
Sizeable Armies
With an army which conforms to the uniqueness of different areas of the country, we can more easily recruit. Let us begin a recruitment drive in remote villages where tradition is strong.
Xat Hao Mee Hte Ja
Our army has become greater and greater. Let us hold a massive parade in the capital to celebrate and display our prowess.
Constitutional States

A New Democracy

Promote Buddhist Values

The People First
