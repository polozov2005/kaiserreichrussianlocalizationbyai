Nasīr's Reforms
The policies of Masqud Shah, that of exorbitantly high taxes and corvée labour, are so reviled by the people of Kumul that any progress towards ending them will be massively popular.
The 21 Begs
The 21 Begs, an assembly of the wealthiest Uyghur landowners in Kumul, have been pressuring Nasīr to take greater charge of the treasury in Kumul. The Begs wish for an updated census to reduce their tax burden.
Re-Ordering Taxes
The first demand of the Begs, the census and tax reform, can be done. Once a census of all tenants, free men and livestock is done, several forms of income, such as halal meat production and machine working can be exempt from taxation.
Opening Autonomy
Masqud Shah thought he could tighten his grip on power by closing the city to immigrants without Qing's authorisation. The Begs ask that additional Uyghur citizens be admitted into the city, and they be treated as free men.
Minor Administration Reforms
The palace in Kumul is staffed by totally unqualified bureaucrats. Masqud Shah did not employ anybody with an Imperial Examination score. By simply asking our administrators to seek certification, we can weed out the worst hangers-on.
Adjusting the Advisory
The power of the two Advisors has always been a risk to the Khanate. Especially with Hoja-Niyaz. If we were to deploy the advisors to the city walls, we could possibly slip reforms past them.
The 30 Begs
With the additional Uyghur people coming to Kumul, it is beneficial if we add nine more men to the graft. Thirty Begs may be unable to break a tie, but we can benefit from their advice.
The Keys to Kumul
The power of the garrison in Kumul has rested with the Advisors for decades now. The Begs promise that their Uyghur levies can man the city walls without extorting the people so heavily. This would alleviate our worries.
The Kind Hand
Reforming the image of the Khan of Kumul requires constant support from the Begs. Through a more dignified appearance, adherence to Muslim customs, and the Begs' word of mouth, Nasīr Shah can be redeemed.
Crush the Corvée
The use of corvée labour to keep land plots orderly and maintain the city was Masqud's idea. Under traditional Islamic jurisprudence and Chinese civil law, corvée labour should not be used when the crimes of the labourers do not justify it.
Increased Constitutionalism
The wide variety of political constitutions that float around southern and eastern China have made it to Kumul. At the very least, they can serve as a basis for writing our future laws in a modern way.
The 2 Advisors
The two Advisor-Generals, Yulbars Khan and Hoja-Niyaz, have been pressuring Nasīr to protect his demesne. To begin, radical leftists must be listed and checked upon within Kumul. Just to be sure.
Adjusting Powers
Masqud Shah left behind a provisional role for his military advisors, doling out their roles based on his whim. A more concrete role of the Advisors will allow Yulbars Khan and Hoja-Niyaz to protect Kumul.
Hoja-Niyaz's Ideals
Hoja-Niyaz, during his failed revolt against Nasīr's father, recalls his fighters fought better when they were supervised by an Imam and set aside time to pray each day. This policy, Hoja-Niyaz believes, will make the defenders of Kumul fight with the spirit of the warriors of old.
Side-lining the Begs
The 21 Begs of Kumul are a relic of Masqud Shah's loneliness and feeling of alienation from fellow Muslims due to his drunkenness. It is best if the Advisors met with Nasīr without consulting the Begs first.
Yulbars' Doctrine
Yulbars Khan, having served the Khanate since the days of Nasīr's grandfather, has enough experience to be tapped into for reform suggestions. Yulbars Khan believes that converting soldier pay from silver to goods will engender more dependent soldiers.
Lowering Taxation
Another area of Yulbars Khan's reforms include lowering taxes on soldiers in our army, to encourage more to join, though it will mean slightly less tax revenue.
Kumul Cavalry Regiment
The Advisors recommend the formation of a provisional Cavalry Regiment, based on the Hui Regiments. The advisors claim this will allow Nasīr to quickly end revolts that appear in Kumul from time to time.
Maintaining the Khan's Powers
The Advisors project an image of Nasīr as a benevolent dictator who will protect Kumul from the waves of uncertainty Chinese bring. At the same time, they depict Nasīr as a victorious Chagatai like those that beat Tibet.
Maintaining the Corvée
Though it is cruel, the Advisors have justified the punishment of forced labour outside Islamic jurisprudence by claiming the criminals are on the verge of becoming fajir, stating that this work will tire out their energy to commit crimes.
The Soft Fist
The Advisors have begun to expand their military policies into the other classes in Kumul. Those citizens who encounter them recognise Nasīr as a pious and safe departure from Masqud, to the people's splendour.
Xinjiang Administration

New Taxes
The garrison's first request is to see the promised tribute to the Qing government. The tribute is obviously not there, and so the garrison has forced us to increase taxes on city people. This will infuriate the Uyghurs without a doubt.
Hire More Tax Collectors
The garrison has chastised our tax collectors, who are still holding out for the hope that Nasīr will be released. We must now take in the garrison's suggested tax collectors, all of whom are Chinese and will extort the Uyghurs mercilessly.
Even Higher Taxes!

Seize Nobles' Money
The Begs of the Khanate are being sought out by the garrison for additional taxation, under the auspices of national security. The families of the Begs are now in hiding with their gold, as both are targets of the garrison's grabbing hand.
Punish the Opposition

Rebuilding Xinjiang

Expand the Kumul Road

Nascent Industry

Military Expansion
The defensive divisions that were bolstered during our war must be replaced. By taking our time, we will be able to plan a larger and safer military for future conflicts.
Civilian Investments
The devastation brought to Kumul shook every home. It would certainly be beneficial if we spend some time rebuilding forges despoiled.
New Manufactures

In order to administer the vastness of Xinjiang, we must defer to the same autonomous principle Governor Yang Zengxin afforded us.
