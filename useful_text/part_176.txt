The Great Depression
The Great Depression which has gripped America for over a decade gradually improved under the stewardship of Al Smith, but Smith's personal reluctance for radical reform and an inability to pass significant reform through Congress made any such progress painfully slow. But now, the situation has been blown apart. Black Monday, the collapse of the Berlin Stock Market in February, has resulted in a renewed wave of economic damage that is spreading throughout American society. Small businesses, already struggling in the inclement economic climate, fall bankrupt by the thousands as it appears any progress made since 1931 has been wiped out in a matter of days and weeks. Rightly or wrongly, fingers are being pointed at the White House, and whoever sits within it come 1937 has a seemingly impossible problem to solve.
